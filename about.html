About

WHO WE ARE

Since 1974, Freedom House Recovery Center has been committed to promote, enhance and support recovery for men, women and children affected by mental illness, developmental disabilities and substance abuse by utilizing an evidence-based, comprehensive and person-centered approach.

Our Story

From our beginning as a social setting detox and halfway house in Chapel Hill, we have consistently grown to address the critical and changing needs of the North Carolinians in our service area by expanding both our services and geographic reach through innovative programs and partnerships. We hold a strong belief that support for people in need is support for positive change throughout our entire community; not only for those who have lost their way through substance use disorder and seek to find it again, or for people challenged by mental illnesses, but for all of us who will benefit from a healthy, productive, and self-sustaining community.

LEADING THE WAY

Freedom House is a Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinic (CCBHC) and serves as a “safety net” clinic for the community. The outpatient clinic provides individualized and community-based services to adults and children who have substance use diagnosis, mental illness, or those who are dually diagnosed. We offer a variety of programs geared toward meeting consumer's behavioral health needs. In addition to routine mental health and substance use therapy and medication management for adults and children, Freedom House also provides Medication Assisted Treatment for those with Opioid Use Disorder, Peer Support Services that allow those with lived recovery experience to support our clientele as they navigate their recovery plan at home and in the community

A STRONGER NETWORK OF CARE

Illness Management and Recovery groups to assist consumers in managing their overall mental health and many other evidence-based therapies with proven outcomes. Mobile Crisis Services are available for all ages and disability groups, as well as walk-in crisis services in each of our outpatient clinics. Through our 16-bed Facility-Based Crisis Unit in Chapel Hill we serve adults who need immediate support for acute substance use and/or mental health emergencies. We also have a men's and women's substance use halfway house for adults in Orange County and Durham County and a men's substance use halfway house in Warren County
